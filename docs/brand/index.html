Brand essence

Precise, fast, premium.

FlexiWires compares every cross-border wire rail on one screen. The brand is engineered and calm with one decisive accent — a signature green that marks the moment of action.

Engineered

Monospace data, exact figures, clean grids. It should feel like a tool built by people who move money.

Calm

Generous space, muted neutrals, a cream canvas. Green is rationed so it always means something.

Expressive

An italic serif accent line cuts through the sans headings — a human note in an engineered system.

Design system

The reasoning, not just the rules

Every choice below is tied back to one goal: make a cross-border money tool feel precise, fast and trustworthy. Here is what we picked, why we picked it, and how it supports the FlexiWires product.

Typography · rationale

Why these three typefaces

A treasury product lives on numbers, scanning and trust. The type system is built around that: a neutral workhorse, a precise data face, and one expressive accent so it never feels cold.

InterUI & Headings · Sans

Inter is a screen-first neutral sans with a tall x-height and excellent legibility at small sizes — designed specifically for interfaces.

  • Why: a fintech dashboard is read fast and often; Inter stays clear from 12px labels to 64px hero headlines.
  • Supports the product: the same face works in marketing and in-app, so the landing page and the real product feel like one thing.
  • Validation: proven at interface scale by Stripe-era fintech, GitHub, Figma; huge weight range (300–700) covers every hierarchy need.
Weights 300–700Tight tracking ≥ 32pxx-height: tall
Instrument SerifAccent · Italic serif

A high-contrast italic serif used only for the accent line inside a heading — e.g. “all from your phone.”

  • Why: pure sans reads efficient but impersonal. One italic serif line adds a human, editorial, premium note — the “routed.” / “your phone” moments.
  • Supports the product: money is emotional; the serif signals craft and confidence, separating FlexiWires from generic SaaS templates.
  • Validation: the sans-for-structure + serif-for-emotion pairing is a current premium-brand pattern; used sparingly it adds character without hurting legibility.
Italic onlyAccent lines & emphasisNever for body
JetBrains MonoData & Labels · Mono

A monospace face for figures, rates, corridor labels and eyebrows — anything that is data or a system label.

  • Why: monospace makes numbers line up and read as “real data,” and tabular figures keep rates like 17.4521 stable as they update.
  • Supports the product: FX is all about comparing numbers; mono visually says “engineered, exact, machine-accurate.”
  • Validation: mono-for-data is the convention across trading, dev and fintech tools; wide-tracked uppercase mono is an instantly-readable label style.

Color · rationale

Why this palette

A restrained, premium base with a single decisive accent — so color always carries meaning instead of decoration.

Signature Green#3FE864 · Accent

One bright, optimistic green reserved for action and confirmation.

  • Why: green reads as “go / success / money saved” — exactly the feeling at the moment of sending. Rationing it to CTAs, status dots and the live indicator makes every green pixel meaningful.
  • Supports the product: users instantly learn “green = the next step / a positive outcome,” which speeds the core flow (quote → lock → send).
  • Validation: against the near-black ink and cream the green hits a strong, lively contrast while pairing with black text on the button for AA legibility.
Cream + Ink#FAF8F4 / #0A0A0A

Warm off-white canvas with near-black text instead of pure white/black.

  • Why: cream feels premium and editorial (less clinical than #FFF); near-black softens harsh pure-black contrast for comfortable long reading.
  • Supports the product: a calm canvas lets the data and the single green accent stand out — critical when the screen is full of rates and numbers.
Hero Black#111214 · Dark panels

A soft near-black for the hero card, the corridor rail and the app section.

  • Why: dark inset panels create rhythm and focus, framing the product shots and making the green pop hardest where it matters.
  • Supports the product: alternating cream and dark sections guide the eye down the page and spotlight key moments (hero, live corridors, get-the-app).
Iconography

Line icons, one weight

Icons are thin, geometric line icons (Lucide family) — never filled, never multi-color.

StyleOutline · 1.75 stroke
  • Why: outline icons match the engineered, lightweight feel of the type and keep the UI calm; a single 1.75px stroke reads consistent at every size.
  • Supports the product: a clean, neutral icon set scales across product features (rails, compliance, alerts) without visual noise competing with the data.
  • Validation: Lucide is open-source, exhaustive and pixel-consistent — safe to grow the product on without redrawing icons.
No fillscurrentColorAccent green only for status/checks
Imagery

Real product, real hands

Imagery is the actual app on real devices in real hands — not abstract illustration or stock office photos.

DirectionProduct-led photography
  • Why: showing the real FlexiWires screen on a phone proves the product exists and works — trust is everything when you handle people’s money.
  • Supports the product: hero (phone on a stone pedestal) and the app section (phone in hand) both put the live interface front-and-center, so visitors see exactly what they’ll use.
  • Treatment: clean cut-outs, soft drop-shadows, neutral/mint or dark backdrops; the device screen carries the only dense color (the app UI).

Do

  • Use real app screens
  • Keep backgrounds calm (cream, mint, dark)
  • Soft realistic shadows
  • One focal device per panel

Don't

  • Generic stock “business people”
  • Busy or colorful backdrops
  • Hard cut-outs with no shadow
  • Recolor or distort the app UI
Motion & layout · rationale

Restrained, purposeful motion

PrinciplesWhy it moves
  • Continuous corridor rail: a always-moving marquee of live corridors signals an active, real-time network — reinforcing “12 partner rails, live.”
  • 3D button text-roll: a small premium flourish on CTAs that rewards interaction without distracting from the task.
  • Reveal-on-scroll: gentle fade/translate as sections enter — guides attention and adds polish; respects prefers-reduced-motion.
  • Generous radius & spacing: 32px panels and roomy vertical rhythm make a data-dense product feel calm and premium, not cramped.
